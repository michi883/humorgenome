What's inside

Each joke is dissected like a scientific specimen — but one that makes you laugh. Our annotations capture not just what's funny, but why and how.

Sample annotation

{
  "text": "I told my wife she was drawing her eyebrows too high. She looked surprised.",
  "category": "Wordplay",
  "layers": ["Content", "Delivery"],
  "rationale": "Double meaning of 'looked surprised' created by exaggerated eyebrows; timing supports misdirection"
}

Benchmark item

  1. Q: Why is this joke funny?
  2. A: (b) The phrase "looked surprised" is a pun caused by high‑drawn eyebrows.

Scoring rubric: mechanism recognition, layer attribution, rationale quality.

Impact & Use Cases

Platforms

Smarter content, safer spaces

Stop the endless whac-a-mole of content moderation. Humor-aware systems understand context — distinguishing clever wordplay from harmful content, detecting sarcasm vs. sincerity.

Researchers

End the anecdotal era

No more "my model seems funnier." Standardized tasks, reproducible metrics, and cultural sensitivity scores that actually measure what matters.

Creators

Your craft, understood

Finally, AI that gets the difference between a callback and a punchline. Systems that recognize timing, appreciate deadpan, and understand why silence can be the funniest response.

Community

Open, credit-first

Comedy belongs to everyone. Our taxonomy, datasets, and benchmarks are open-source — transparent, improvable, and owned by the community that creates humor.

Roadmap Ahead

We're moving fast because humor can't wait. Every day, AI systems make millions of decisions about comedic content — most without understanding what makes something actually funny.

  1. MVD v0.2: expand categories; add 500→1k scientifically annotated specimens; refine rubric with inter-annotator agreement scores.
  2. Benchmark v1.0: public tasks + baseline runs; lightweight leaderboard with cultural sensitivity metrics.
  3. Culture packs: region/language-specific variants with community maintainers (framework designed for 100K+ jokes across 50+ languages).
  4. Pilots: partner with AI labs & media platforms to test engagement + safety lift in real-world deployments.
